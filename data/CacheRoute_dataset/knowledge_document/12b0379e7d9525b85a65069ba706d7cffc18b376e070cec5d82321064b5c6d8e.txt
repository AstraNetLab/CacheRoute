The list of national anthem performers at the Super Bowl documents the
singers and musicians who have performed The Star Spangled Banner before
the championship game of the National Football League and traces one of
the most visible ceremonial traditions in American sports. Since the
first Super Bowl in 1967 the anthem has served as a symbolic opening to
the game and has become a major pregame television moment watched by
audiences across the United States and around the world. Over the
decades the National Football League has invited solo singers choirs
bands and collaborative acts to perform the anthem, creating a long
historical record that reflects changes in music, media, and public
culture. Among the most famous performers are Whitney Houston, whose
rendition at Super Bowl XXV in 1991 is widely remembered as one of the
most acclaimed anthem performances in sports history, as well as Billy
Joel, Diana Ross, Mariah Carey, Beyoncé, Alicia Keys, Lady Gaga, Pink,
Gladys Knight, Demi Lovato, Jazmine Sullivan and Eric Church, Mickey
Guyton, Chris Stapleton, Reba McEntire, and Jon Batiste. Whitney
Houston’s performance became especially iconic because of its emotional
effect and its continued circulation in later media commentary. In more
recent years the anthem tradition has remained highly visible, with
Chris Stapleton performing at Super Bowl LVII in 2023, Reba McEntire
performing at Super Bowl LVIII in 2024, Jon Batiste performing at Super
Bowl LIX in 2025, and Charlie Puth announced for Super Bowl LX in 2026.
These recent selections show how the NFL continues to choose nationally
prominent artists from different genres including country, pop, soul,
rhythm and blues, and contemporary singer songwriter traditions. The
anthem performance is usually accompanied by other ceremonial elements
such as color guards, military flyovers, and sign language
interpretation, which together create a carefully staged pregame
sequence before kickoff. Media coverage often treats the anthem as one
of the central cultural moments of the Super Bowl broadcast alongside
the halftime show and the advertisements, and commentators frequently
compare tempo, vocal arrangement, style, and emotional impact across
years. The full list of performers therefore offers more than a sequence
of names; it also shows how the Super Bowl developed into a national
entertainment ritual that combines sport, television spectacle,
patriotism, celebrity culture, and live music performance. Because the
event is renewed every year, the list of national anthem performers at
the Super Bowl continues to grow and remains a useful reference point
for understanding both NFL history and the changing landscape of
