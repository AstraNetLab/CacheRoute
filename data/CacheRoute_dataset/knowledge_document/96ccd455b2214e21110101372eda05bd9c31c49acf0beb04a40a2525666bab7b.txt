Tiger Woods is widely regarded as one of the greatest golfers in the
history of the sport and the list of career achievements by Tiger Woods
represents one of the most influential records in modern professional
golf. Eldrick Tont Woods was born in 1975 in Cypress California and
emerged as a child prodigy whose early television appearances and junior
golf victories attracted national attention. His amateur career already
included three consecutive United States Amateur Championship victories
which established him as one of the most accomplished amateur players
ever before turning professional. Woods turned professional in 1996 and
quickly transformed the landscape of professional golf with a
combination of athletic power technical precision and intense
competitive focus. One of the earliest defining moments in his career
came at the 1997 Masters Tournament where he won his first major
championship at Augusta National Golf Club and did so by a record margin
of twelve strokes while also becoming the youngest Masters champion in
history. This victory marked the beginning of a dominant era in which
Woods became the central figure in global golf. Over the following
decade Woods accumulated an extraordinary number of tournament victories
on the PGA Tour including multiple wins at the Masters Tournament the
PGA Championship the United States Open and The Open Championship which
together form the four major championships in professional golf. His
performance at the 2000 United States Open at Pebble Beach is widely
considered one of the most dominant major championship performances ever
recorded because Woods finished fifteen strokes ahead of the field and
produced a score that set several historical records. In the same era
Woods achieved what became known as the Tiger Slam when he
simultaneously held all four major titles across the 2000 and 2001
seasons by winning the 2000 United States Open the 2000 Open
Championship the 2000 PGA Championship and the 2001 Masters Tournament.
This accomplishment demonstrated an unprecedented level of sustained
excellence in modern golf and further solidified Woods as the leading
player of his generation. Throughout the early 2000s Woods remained
ranked number one in the Official World Golf Ranking for record breaking
stretches and accumulated dozens of PGA Tour victories including
multiple season long dominance periods where he led the tour in scoring
average victories and earnings. His rivalry with other leading players
and his consistent success in major championships helped increase
television ratings sponsorship investment and international interest in
professional golf which many commentators refer to as the Tiger Woods
effect. Woods also won numerous prestigious awards including multiple
PGA Player of the Year honors and several Byron Nelson Awards
recognizing the lowest scoring average on the PGA Tour. Beyond his
tournament victories Woods also contributed to golf through innovations
in athletic training strength conditioning and mental preparation which
influenced how modern golfers approach professional competition. After a
period of injuries and personal challenges in the late 2000s and early
2010s Woods staged one of the most celebrated comebacks in sports
history when he won the 2019 Masters Tournament for his fifteenth major
championship after an eleven year major title drought. The victory at
Augusta National was widely celebrated by fans players and commentators
and became one of the most watched moments in modern golf broadcasting.
In addition to his competitive achievements Woods has also been involved
in philanthropic initiatives including the TGR Foundation which focuses
on educational opportunities and youth development programs. The
complete list of career achievements by Tiger Woods therefore includes
major championship victories PGA Tour titles world ranking records award
recognitions and lasting influence on the popularity and athletic
evolution of professional golf. Even in the 2020s Woods continues to be
referenced in discussions of golf history competitive excellence and the
global growth of the sport as younger players build their careers within
a professional environment that was transformed by his extraordinary
